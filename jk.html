Tribal Dances of Jharkhand

Jharkhand, a state known for its rich tribal culture, is home to numerous tribal communities like the Santhal, Oraon, Munda, and Ho. The dances of Jharkhand are vibrant, often performed during agricultural festivals, weddings, and religious occasions.

Santhal Dance

The Santal tribal dance, while not specifically a prominent dance form in Himachal Pradesh, can be found in other parts of India, especially where the Santhal tribe is present, such as in states like Jharkhand, Bihar, and West Bengal. The Santhal dance, also known as the Jhika Dasain dance, is typically performed during festivals like Dasain (Dussehra) and Sohrai. Santhal dance is a traditional dance of the Santhal tribe, performed to celebrate harvest and other important events.

Significance: The dance reflects the close bond between the Santhal tribe and nature.

Style: Dancers perform in a circle with rhythmic foot movements and hand gestures.

Music and Instruments: The dance is often accompanied by drums (like Tamak and Tumdak), Madal (a cylindrical drum), Nagade (a hemispherical drum), and cymbals.

Costumes: Performers wear traditional Santhal attire, which includes distinct patterns and wide borders on clothing like sarees and dhotis.

It's primarily a women-centric dance performed during festivals like Karam and Jitiya. Costume: Dancers wear traditional attire, often decorated with beads and feathers.

Style: The dance is performed in a slow, graceful manner, with dancers swaying and moving in circles.

Group Dynamics: Dancers form a linear line, holding hands, and move in a circular fashion.

Festivals: It's a common sight during harvest festivals (Karam and Jitiya).

Regional Variations: The dance has different names and styles depending on the region and occasion, such as Angnai, Chandhantari, and various others.

Oral Tradition: Jhumair is a deeply rooted oral tradition, with songs and dances passed down through generations.
