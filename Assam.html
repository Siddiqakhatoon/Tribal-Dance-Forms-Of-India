Tribal Dances of Assam

Bihu Dance

Assam is a state in northeastern India known for its wildlife, archeological sites and tea plantations.Folk dances of Assam include the Bihu and the Bagurumba (both danced during festivals held in the spring), the Bhortal, the Ojapali dance.

Bihu is a vibrant and popular folk dance of Assam, India, deeply rooted in the state's cultural heritage and agricultural traditions. It's a key part of the Bihu festival, celebrated three times a year, and involves lively dance movements, traditional clothing, and musical instruments. Bihu dances are performed by young men and women, often expressing joy, love, and connection to the agricultural cycle.

Celebration and Tradition: Bihu is an integral part of the Bihu festival, a time for celebrating the harvest, welcoming spring, and thanking the gods for prosperity.

Dance Styles: The dance is characterized by brisk steps, rapid hand movements, and lively expressions of joy.

Costumes: Dancers wear traditional Assamese clothing, often featuring vibrant colors and motifs.

Music: The dance is accompanied by musical instruments like "Dhol" (a large drum), Pepa (a wind instrument), and other traditional instruments.

Themes: Bihu dances often revolve around themes of love, harvest, and the joy of life.

Bihu Dance: A Reflection of Assamese Culture The Bihu dance is not just a dance; it's a way of expressing joy, celebrating the harvest, and strengthening community bonds. It's a living tradition that continues to be passed down through generations, reminding Assamese people of their rich cultural heritage and the importance of their agricultural roots.

Bagurumba Dance

The Bagurumba is a traditional folk dance of the Bodo people of Assam, also known as the "butterfly dance" due to its gentle, swaying movements. It's a cultural dance performed during the Bwisagu festival, a Bodo New Year celebration, and is believed to be inspired by nature. The dance is typically performed by women dressed in colorful attire and is accompanied by traditional musical instruments.

Characteristics: Inspired by Nature: The Bagurumba dance is believed to be influenced by the gentle movements of butterflies and birds, reflecting the Bodo people's close connection to nature.

Performed by Women: The dance is traditionally performed by women, who move with graceful and flowing steps.

Colorful Attire: The dancers wear vibrant, colorful traditional attire, including dokhna, jwmgra (fasra), and aronai.

Musical Accompaniment: Traditional musical instruments like the serja (a bowed instrument), sifung (a long flute), and kham (a long drum) accompany the dance.
